# Computational Physics Portfolio

A collection of computational physics simulations and numerical modeling projects developed in Python. These projects explore physical systems through numerical methods, stochastic simulation, and scientific visualization.

---

## About Me

I am a third-year undergraduate student studying **Physics** and **Applied Mathematics** at **California State University, San Bernardino**. My academic interests include computational physics, condensed matter physics, dynamical systems, and numerical modeling of physical systems.

This repository contains projects developed through coursework and independent exploration in computational physics. The goal of these projects is to apply numerical methods and programming to simulate and analyze physical phenomena that are difficult or impossible to solve analytically.

## Featured Projects

### Triple Pendulum Simulation

A numerical simulation of a **triple pendulum**, a nonlinear dynamical system known for chaotic motion. The system is modeled using coupled differential equations and integrated numerically to observe complex time evolution.

Concepts explored:

* Nonlinear dynamics
* Coupled differential equations
* Chaotic motion
* Numerical ODE integration
* Scientific animation

The simulation visualizes the motion of the pendulum system and highlights how small changes in initial conditions can produce dramatically different trajectories.

---

### 3D Random Walk Simulation

A stochastic simulation of random walks in three dimensions. This project investigates how displacement scales with the number of steps and demonstrates statistical properties of random motion.

Concepts explored:

* Random processes
* Diffusion behavior
* Root-mean-square displacement
* Statistical physics concepts
* Monte Carlo-style simulations

The simulation also explores how average displacement grows as the square root of the number of steps.

---

### Projectile Motion with Drag

A numerical simulation of projectile motion that includes **air resistance**. The model compares ideal projectile motion with more realistic trajectories when drag forces are present.

Concepts explored:

* Newtonian mechanics
* Drag forces
* Numerical integration
* Central difference approximations
* Trajectory visualization

This project demonstrates how computational methods allow us to analyze systems where closed-form analytic solutions are difficult.

---

### Radioactive Decay Simulation

A probabilistic simulation of radioactive decay processes. The model illustrates how large numbers of unstable nuclei decay over time and how statistical fluctuations arise in finite samples.

Concepts explored:

* Exponential decay
* Probability and statistics in physics
* Time evolution of particle populations
* Simulation of stochastic processes

---

### Point Charge Spring Simulation

A numerical model combining electrostatic forces and spring dynamics. The system demonstrates how multiple physical forces can be incorporated into a computational model.

Concepts explored:

* Electrostatics
* Oscillatory motion
* Numerical force integration
* Euler methods
